# 2. REPEATED & LINKED QUESTIONS

## Key PYQ Linkage Patterns Identified from Files

### Pattern 1: Options of Previous Years Become Questions
UPSC frequently picks **incorrect options or distractor details** from past questions and frames them as standalone questions in subsequent years.

### Pattern 2: Explanations/Answers Spawn New Questions
The **factual content in explanations** of PYQ answers often becomes the basis for new questions. For example, when an explanation mentions a specific Act or treaty in passing, that detail appears as a direct question 2–3 years later.

### Pattern 3: Theme Recycling with Angle Change
The same sub-theme is asked repeatedly with a different twist.

### Subject-wise Linked/Repeated Question Clusters

#### ANCIENT & MEDIEVAL HISTORY
- **Vijayanagar Empire:** Asked in 2017 (Kakatiya foreign trade port Motupalli), 2013 (related Kakatiya Q), and again in later years — UPSC loves Vijayanagar economy + foreign trade
- **Mughal Art & Culture:** Asked 2+ times, with Persian wheel irrigation twisted into a new question format
- **Ashoka Inscriptions:** Theme asked in 2013, 2015, and later years — location + content
- **Buddhism concepts:** Bodhisattva concept asked in options across 2013, 2014, 2017, and as direct questions later
- **Mahayana vs Hinayana:** Asked repeatedly across Art & Culture and Ancient History

#### MODERN HISTORY
- **Education Reforms (British):** Asked 4 times — "UPSC has asked several questions from this particular theme/sub-theme" (noted in PYQ file)
- **Swadeshi Movement:** 3 direct questions + appears in options of other questions
- **Quit India Movement:** Date, events, timeline — asked 4 times with different angles
- **Gandhi's phases/Satyagraha:** Champaran (1917), Salt March — recycled themes
- **Cripps Mission:** Asked twice directly, referenced in other questions

#### ENVIRONMENT & ECOLOGY
- **Eco-Engineering:** "UPSC asks standard conceptual questions on Eco-engineering every year"

# 3. OPTIONAL SUBJECT RECOMMENDATION

## Comparative Analysis: Anthropology vs Sociology vs Public Administration

| Parameter | Anthropology | Sociology | Pub Administration |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Syllabus Size** | Medium-Small | Medium-Small | Medium |
| **GS Overlap** | Low-Medium (GS1 society) | HIGH (GS1, GS2, GS4, Essay) | HIGH (GS2, GS4) |
| **Avg Marks (Recent)** | 260–280 (declining) | 270–290 | 250–270 |
| **Preparation Time** | 3–4 months | 3–4 months | 4–5 months |
| **Engineer-friendliness** | Moderate | HIGH | HIGH |
| **Answer Writing** | Conceptual + diagrams | Analytical + current affairs | Factual + case-based |
| **Revision Ease** | Easy (limited syllabus) | Easy (overlaps with GS) | Moderate |
| **Current Trend (2024-25)** | Overpopulated, scoring DOWN | Stable, slight dip in CSE-24 | Stable but lower ceiling |
| **Topper Preference** | Was popular, now declining | Rising (AIR 4, 5 in 2024) | Consistent mid-tier |
| **Risk Factor** | HIGH (unpredictable scoring) | LOW-MEDIUM | LOW |

## ✅ RECOMMENDATION: SOCIOLOGY

**Reasons:**
1. **Maximum GS overlap** — Sociology Paper II (Indian Society) directly helps GS1 (Indian Society, Social Issues), GS2 (Governance, Social Justice), GS4 (Ethics — thinkers, values), and Essay Paper. You effectively prepare for 3 papers while studying 1.
2. **Engineer-friendly** — logical frameworks, sociological theories are systematic and appeal to analytical minds. Karl Marx → class struggle → can be linked to economic models. Weber's bureaucracy → directly useful for GS2.
3. **Limited syllabus, faster revision** — can be covered in 3–3.5 months with daily 1–1.5 hour investment.
4. **Current affairs integration** — every social issue (caste, gender, urbanization, communalism) is both current affairs AND sociology. Double benefit.
5. **Answer writing advantage** — sociology answers can use diagrams, flowcharts, and models — plays to your engineering strength.
6. **Anthropology risk** — Expert analysis notes Anthropology's "longer downturn" as an "over-populated" subject with declining average scores. Not worth the gamble with only 2 attempts left.
7. **Pub Ad limitation** — more factual, less analytical. Lower scoring ceiling. More committees/commissions to memorize.

# 5. LOW-ROI TOPICS TO DEPRIORITIZE

These are in the syllabus but have extremely low or zero question frequency. Don't spend more than a surface-level read.

### Deprioritize (Don't Skip Entirely, but Minimal Time)
1. **Detailed dynastic chronology** — memorizing every minor dynasty's dates (focus on major: Maurya, Gupta, Mughal, Chola, Vijayanagar)
2. **Obscure archaeological sites** — unless they're recently in news or UNESCO-listed
3. **Detailed chemical formulas/reactions** in Science — UPSC asks application, not theory
4. **Advanced physics concepts** — nuclear physics equations, quantum mechanics math
5. **Minor philosophical schools** beyond Sankhya, Yoga, Vedanta, Nyaya, Buddhism, Jainism
6. **Detailed statistical data** — exact GDP numbers, population figures (focus on trends, not numbers)
7. **Foreign country-specific geography** — unless it's about straits, strategic locations, or resource mapping
8. **Minor tribal movements** beyond Santhal, Munda, Birsa, Kol
9. **Detailed literary criticism** of ancient texts — focus on which text said what, not literary analysis
10. **Obscure government committees** — unless they led to major policy changes
11. **Detailed music theory** (ragas, talas) — only identify dance forms and their states
12. **Detailed world history dates** — focus on themes (colonization, industrial revolution, world wars) not specific dates

### Complete Skip (Not Asked in 15 Years)
- Detailed metallurgy of ancient India
- Specific pottery classification systems
- Minor Buddhist sub-sects beyond Mahayana/Hinayana/Vajrayana
- Detailed river geomorphology terminology
- Obscure international agreements India isn't party to

# 9. SMART EXAM STRATEGY

## Prelims — Elimination & Guessing Framework

### The 60-40 Rule
- **Target:** 60% questions you KNOW → solve first (90 min)
- **Remaining:** 40% use elimination → intelligent guessing (30 min)

### Elimination Techniques
1. **Absolute words are usually wrong:** "always," "never," "only," "all" → likely incorrect option
2. **Extreme options:** If one option is extreme, it's usually wrong
3. **Two similar options:** If 2 options say almost the same thing, both are likely wrong — answer is the different one
4. **"All of the above" tendency:** If 3 options seem correct, "All of the above" is often correct
5. **UPSC's negative framing:** "Which is NOT correct" — read carefully, they test attention
6. **Static facts in options:** If you know 2 out of 3 statements are correct, the answer includes those 2

### Time Management
- **First 90 min:** Attempt all questions you're confident about (aim for 60–70)
- **Next 20 min:** Return to questions where you can eliminate 2 options → attempt
- **Last 10 min:** Review marked questions, don't change answers unless certain

### CSAT Strategy (Qualifying — 33%)
- Don't waste excessive time preparing — it's qualifying
- Focus on comprehension passages (easiest marks)
- Basic math — practice 30 previous year papers
- Decision making — use common sense, pick moderate option
